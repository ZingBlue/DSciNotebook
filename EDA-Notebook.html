Looking deeper - AI Usage for Accessibility Purposes

Because the perceived benefits of AI span a variety of domains, we next focused our analysis on a specific application to better understand how attitudes differ across demographic groups. We selected Robo-vacuums as a case example: an everyday form of AI that can meaningfully improve accessibility, particularly for individuals with mobility limitations or other disabilities. Prior research has noted that assistive cleaning technologies can reduce physical strain and support independent living among vulnerable populations (Cleaning Is Caring, n.d.). By examining responses related to Robo-vacuum use, we explore whether age, gender, and other demographic characteristics influence who recognizes these accessibility-related benefits of AI.
